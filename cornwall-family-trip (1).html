Two-Base Strategy

Cornwall is long — split the trip between two superb YHAs. Start at YHA Treyarnon Bay (north coast, 4.6★ — 30 seconds from the beach, surf, rock pools) for nights 1–2, then move to YHA Boscastle (4.8★ — where the river meets the sea, near Tintagel) for nights 3–4.

Tip: Book Treyarnon Bay well ahead — it's one of the most popular YHAs in England and sells out months in advance for June–July.

Botallack Mine & Engine Houses

The most iconic image in Cornwall — two Victorian engine house stacks clinging impossibly to the cliff face above crashing Atlantic waves. Free to visit. Walk the clifftop path for increasingly dramatic views. Rated 4.9★ by nearly 300 visitors. The mine shafts extended 400m out under the sea.

Cape Cornwall — England's Only Cape

A short walk from Botallack — Cape Cornwall is the only cape in England (Land's End is technically a headland). A dramatic windswept headland with another ruined mine chimney on top. Completely free, wild and utterly spectacular in June sunshine.

A hand-carved open-air Greek theatre built into the granite clifftop above Porthcurno Beach — one of the most extraordinary man-made structures in Britain. Admission includes a small museum about its remarkable creator Rowena Cade. Check for evening performances in June.

Insider Advice

Cornwall Know-How

  • The Night Riviera Sleeper from Paddington to Penzance is a brilliant budget hack — a private cabin for the family, you arrive refreshed, and it often costs less than a day train. Book via GWR.
  • Book YHA Treyarnon Bay as early as possible — it books out months ahead for June–July. It's one of England's most popular hostels for a reason.
  • The First Kernow buses cover most of Cornwall — a Day Rider ticket (~£9 adult, ~£5 child) covers unlimited travel. The A1/A2 routes link Penzance, St Ives, and St Just for the tin mine area.
  • Tintagel is only open Wednesday–Sunday — plan your Day 4 accordingly, and book English Heritage tickets online (cheaper than gate price). An EH family membership pays for itself on this trip.
  • Check tide times for Kynance Cove religiously — arriving at high tide means no beach. The Lizard outpost of the NT has up-to-date advice. Aim for 2 hours before low tide.
  • A real Cornish pasty has a crimped edge on the side (not the top), contains beef skirt, potato, swede and onion — and jam goes first, then cream (the opposite of Devon). Cornish Pasty Association has a maker finder.
  • Botallack and Cape Cornwall are completely free and genuinely more spectacular than Land's End (which is commercialised and charges entry). Skip Land's End entirely.
